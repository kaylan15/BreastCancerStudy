Breast Cancer Survival Study

Overall Introduction

Amidst rapid globalization in today’s world, the need for processing and interpreting biological data has skyrocketed. More specifically, cancer (or oncology) research is a popular area of study, with significant funding provided by the government, charities, and private corporations. In 2020 alone, breast cancer became the most commonly diagnosed cancer worldwide, with over 2.2 million new cases reported. This study aims to analyze breast cancer trends and survival factors through data visualization.

Introduction to the Data

The dataset, titled “Breast Cancer” and obtained from Kaggle, includes approximately 4024 rows of breast cancer-related information. It contains 16 attributes representing aspects that may affect breast cancer diagnosis and survival. The table below summarizes these variables:

Variable Name Details
Age Age number
Race Race (White, Black, Other)
Marital_status Marital status (Married, Single, Other)
T_Stage Indicates increasing size and/or extent of the tumor
N_Stage Describes whether the cancer has spread to nearby areas and how extensively
6th_Stage Breast Adjusted AJCC 6th Stage
Differentiate The degree of cellular differentiation
Grade Grade score based on cellular differentiation
A_Stage Regional or distant neoplasm spread
Tumor_Size Exact size of the tumor in millimeters

Interactive Heatmap

The interactive heatmap shows survival trends based on grade and race. For example, discrepancies in survival outcomes appear in grade 4 data, where significant differences exist across racial groups. White patients report a higher survival rate (~67 months) compared to Black patients (~49 months).

Survival Months by Stage

This bar chart shows that as the cancer stage progresses (e.g., IIA to IIIC), average survival months consistently decline, emphasizing the importance of early detection.

Cases by Race

This bar chart represents the total number of breast cancer cases by race. White patients comprise the majority in this dataset. However, this may reflect collection biases rather than higher susceptibility.

Summary and Additional Work

The analysis reveals age, stage, and racial factors significantly impact survival outcomes. Future work could include creating interactive dashboards to explore geospatial trends and risk factors, making breast cancer data more accessible and actionable for diverse audiences.
